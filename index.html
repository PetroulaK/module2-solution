Types of Chocolate


Dark Chocolate contains more than 50% cocoa solids. Dark chocolate can come in a variety of flavors which are categorized by the industry as unsweetened chocolate, bittersweet chocolate and semisweet chocolate.
Dark Chocolate
Milk Chocolate contains between 35% and 55% cocoa solids. Milk chocolate often has a flavor profile that can be described as sweet and chocolatey, with notes of cooked milk and caramelized sugar and a vanilla aftertaste.
Milk Chocolate
White Chocolate contains no cocoa solids, just cocoa butter. Typically, white chocolate is flavoured with vanilla to give it a delicate, slightly floral flavour. As both vanilla and white chocolate have creamy undertones, it makes perfect sense that the two would go well together.
White Chocolate
